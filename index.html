The Yaolympics Chronicles

Each year, a hexad of warriors travels from the far reaches of the world and converge upon the great Yaolympic village of Basking Ridge, NJ. Much as the first Olympics in 776 BCE, each Yaolympics represents a test of physical and mental fortitude as the warriors attempt to throw ping-pong balls into Solo cups, to swim across a 25 meter pool without drowning, and to toss bags filled with dried corn kernels onto an inclined board. The middle of each Yaolympics is punctuated by a modern ekecheiria -- a feast of epic proportions where the flow of Chinese food is certainly endless. To the winning team goes eternal glory, their names etched in the annals of history and the Yaolympics trophy!
